COLLABORATIVE CLOUD

SUMMARY

To fulfil the MetService Challenge, I considered what makes an app 'Smart'. From the guest lecture, there were two aspects of Smart design discussed which appealed to me; connection and an innovation.

With Collaborative Cloud, I aim to produce a weather app which connects users to one another, to provide real time weather updates and help them make more informed decisions for their daily routines. I also want to explore an innovation in technology, with an immersive augmented reality experience.

Home Screen

The default home screen of the app displays the current weather, with the design aesthetic changing based on the time and current weather. A calm, clear day would have a light, pastelly colour palette, whereas a stormy day would feature a dark, grungy palette.

From this screen, the user can access their weather forecast, personal settings and custom circles. Circles are intergrated social media services, which allow users to connect with one another on a private and public basis.

The home screen will also integrate the optional augmented reality interface.



Augmented Reality

The above image shows the proposed Augmented Reality interface. Using your devices camera, the home screen interface will be changed to provide a live feed, to provide context to the information provided by MetService. The user will also have the ability to use their camera to capture images which they can upload to their circles.



Private Circle

Users will have access to two types of circle, public and private.

Private circles are created by connecting to other people known by the user. These personal circles provide a chat interface for all members of a circle to communicate via. Personal circles are intended to allow users to plan events which may be weather dependent e.g. A group of golfers planning the perfect tee time. After an event is created, the app can then provide event alerts to the devices idle screen.

The public circle features gallery of images collected and posted by users, like photo sharing services such as Instagram. Images are sorted based on location to provide users access to a crowd sourced weather feed. Users can change the location which is displayed, so they can compare their local weather.
